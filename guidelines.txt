Global Elements (Site-Wide)

    Persistent Navigation Bar: Logo (left), Anchor links to Services/Process/Case Studies (center), "Book a Call" button (right).

    Floating Action Button: A sticky WhatsApp icon in the bottom corner that opens a direct chat with your own automated lead-qualification bot.

Section 1: The Hero (The First Impression)

    The Hook: A single, powerful headline stating the ultimate benefit of your automation services (focusing on time saved or revenue gained).

    The Sub-hook: A brief explanation of the mechanism (e.g., custom AI chatbots and automated CRMs for modern businesses).

    Primary CTA: A high-contrast button to schedule a discovery call or interact with a live demo bot.

    Visual Asset: A clean dashboard mockup, a dynamic graphic showing apps connecting (like WhatsApp, Instagram, and a database), or a short video of a bot in action.

Section 2: The "Pain" Identification (Why are they here?)

    Structure: A 3-column grid or a sleek checklist.

    Content Focus: Highlight the exact symptoms of a business lacking automation.

        Losing leads because of slow response times on social media.

        Wasting hours on manual data entry into spreadsheets.

        Disorganized customer tracking and missed follow-ups.

Section 3: Core Solutions (What you build)

    Structure: Distinct feature blocks, ideally with a small icon or animation for each.

    Service 1: Smart Chatbot Development. Highlight platforms relevant to your market (WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook) for 24/7 lead capture and customer service.

    Service 2: Custom CRM Architecture. Emphasize building lightweight, highly effective systems (like Airtable setups) that manage data automatically.

    Service 3: Workflow Automation. Explain how you connect their existing tools behind the scenes (using platforms like n8n or Make) to eliminate repetitive tasks.

Section 4: The Lead Magnet (Free Value)

    Structure: A visually distinct banner section.

    The Offer: Give away a high-value resource in exchange for an email or WhatsApp opt-in. A perfect fit here would be a downloadable guide or a mini-course (e.g., "How to build your own AI CRM in 10 minutes").

    The Goal: Build your audience and capture leads who aren't quite ready to buy yet but are interested in tech solutions.

Section 5: Case Studies & Social Proof (Trust Building)

    Structure: A carousel or a grid of success stories.

    Focus: Do not focus on the code; focus on the business result.

    Examples to feature: * Showcase a real estate or hospitality flow (like a WhatsApp bot managing villa rental bookings and FAQs).

        Showcase an e-commerce or service business where an Instagram bot increased ad conversion rates.

    Metrics: Include specific numbers if possible (hours saved, response time reduced).

Section 6: The Process (Setting Expectations)

    Structure: A simple, horizontal 4-step timeline.

    Step 1: Discovery. Mapping the client's bottlenecks.

    Step 2: Strategy. Designing the bot flows and CRM architecture.

    Step 3: Build & Integrate. The actual development phase.

    Step 4: Handover & Support. Training the client to use the new system.

Section 7: Final CTA & Footer

    The Final Push: A large, clean section reiterating the main value proposition with a final "Book a Consultation" calendar embed.

    Footer Links: Privacy policy, terms of service, contact email, and links to your professional social media or WhatsApp community group.
